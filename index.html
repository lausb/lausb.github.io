5. Research

My research works are related to nonlinear functional analysis, variational methods and nonlinear differential equations.

5.1. Lyapunov-Schmidt reduction, critical groups and elliptic resonant problems

Solutions of many nonlinear differential equations can be found as critical points \(u\) of certain functional \(f:X\rightarrow \mathbb {R}\). Using homology theory we can describe local behavior of \(f\) near \(u\) via critical group\[ C_{\ast }(f,u)=H_{\ast }(f_{c},f_{c}\backslash \left \{ u\right \} )\text {,}\] where \(c=f(u)\) and \(f_{c}=f^{-1}(-\infty ,c]\), \(H_{*}\) stands for singular homology with coefficients in \(\mathbb {Q}\). Critical group can be used to distinguish critical points. Global behavior of \(f\) is described via critical group at infinity \(C_{\ast }(f,\infty )\). Their relations are characterized by Morse inequalities.

If \(X\) is a Hilbert space, under Lyapunov-Schmidt reduction condition, there are subspace \(Y\) of \(X\) and \(\varphi :Y\rightarrow \mathbb {R}\) such that \(u\) is critical point of \(f\) if and only if \(v=P_{Y}u\) is critical point of \(\varphi \). So the problem of finding critical points of \(f\) is converted to finding critical points of \(\varphi \).

To combine Morse theory and Lyapunov-Schmidt reduction, the relations between critical groups of \(f\) and \(\varphi \) are crucial. In [15, 30, 35] we proved that under the first L-S reduction\[ C_{\ast }(f,u)\cong C_{\ast }(\varphi ,v)\text {,\qquad }C_{\ast }(f,\infty )\cong C_{\ast }(\varphi ,\infty )\text {;}\] while under the second L-S reduction, if \(j=\operatorname *{codim}Y<\infty \), then\[ C_{\ast }(f,u)\cong C_{\ast -j}(\varphi ,v)\text {,\qquad }C_{\ast }(f,\infty )\cong C_{\ast -j}(\varphi ,\infty )\text {.}\]

In the above papers and [26, 29], we applied the above results and ideas in Morse theory to obtain multiple solutions of elliptic resonant problems such as \[ \Delta u+p(u)=0\text {,\qquad }u\in H_{0}^{1}(\Omega )\text {,}\] where \(\Omega \) is a bounded smooth domain in \(\mathbb {R}^{N}\), \(p\in C(\mathbb {R})\) is asymptotically linear with resonance at an eigenvalue \(\lambda _{k}\) of the Laplacian operator,\[ \lambda :=\lim _{\left \vert t\right \vert \rightarrow \infty }\frac {p(t)}{t}=\lambda _{k}\text {.}\] Our results improve those of S.J. Li, M. Willem, Z.T. Zhang, and others. (The nonresonant case \(\lambda \neq \lambda _{k}\) is easy thus not interesting.)
